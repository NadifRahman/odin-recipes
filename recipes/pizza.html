Pizza

What is Pizza?

Pizza is a beloved Italian dish that has gained immense popularity across the globe. It consists of a round, flat base of dough, which is topped with a tomato sauce, cheese, and a variety of other ingredients such as meats, vegetables, and herbs. The dough is then baked to perfection, resulting in a deliciously crispy and flavorful crust. Originating from Naples, Italy, pizza has evolved into numerous styles and variations, from the thin and crispy Neapolitan pizza to the thick and hearty Chicago-style deep-dish. Its versatility and universal appeal have made pizza a staple in many cultures, celebrated for its simplicity and the endless possibilities of toppings.

Ingredients

  • Pizza dough (either homemade or store-bought)
  • Tomato sauce (marinara or pizza sauce)
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Pepperoni slices
  • Bell peppers, sliced
  • Red onion, thinly sliced
  • Mushrooms, sliced
  • Black olives, sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra virgin olive oil

Steps

  2. Roll out the pizza dough on a floured surface to your desired thickness.
  3. Place the rolled-out dough onto a pizza stone or a baking sheet.
  4. Spread tomato sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges for the crust.
  5. Sprinkle a generous amount of mozzarella cheese over the sauce.
  6. Add your desired toppings, such as pepperoni, bell peppers, onions, mushrooms, and olives.
  7. Drizzle a little olive oil over the pizza and add a pinch of salt and other seasonings if desired.
  8. Place the pizza in the oven and bake for about 10-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden and cheese is bubbly.
  9. Once the pizza is cooked, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes.
  10. Garnish with fresh basil leaves before slicing and serving.
